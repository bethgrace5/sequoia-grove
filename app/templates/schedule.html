Scheduling

When a name is clicked, all instances of that name highlight with an aqua color. Any space that that employee is able to work is highlighted with light green. Additionally, any employee who can work the clicked spot is highlighted in light green in the list on the right.

When a name is placed on the schedule, it currently checks three things. First, that the employee is current. Second, that the employee knows the position the shift requires. Finally, that the employee has availability for the shift they are being scheduled for, including accounting for approved vacation requests. If there are any errors, the name is red, and the

The shifts on the schedule can be arbitrarially ordered, and grouped by placing spacers where desired. When writing the schedule, the shifts can be narrowed to focus on one position at a time. It calcuates the number of days and hours each employee is scheduled for when it is saved. When the schedule is ready, it can be published so it's viewable by employees.
